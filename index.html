About Me

My research focuses on rethinking traditional methods and developing modern frameworks for analyzing biomedical data in the presence of measurement error, high dimensionality, and complexity. To solve these challenges, I develop new statistical and computational methods that: 1) correct for errors in functional and high-dimensional data, 2) integrate bias correction into generalized regression and functional modeling frameworks, and 3) translate them into user-friendly R packages that enable biomedical researchers to apply these tools in practice.

What drives me as a researcher is the belief that rigorous methods are essential to revealing the truth in data. My mission is to develop tools that not only improve the validity of biomedical findings but also make these tools accessible to the wider scientific and public health communities.
