About Me

I am a postdoctoral fellow in Biostatistics at Indiana University, where my research focuses on developing statistical and computational methods for analyzing complex, high-dimensional biomedical data. My work addresses challenges such as measurement error, functional data, missingness, and the integration of wearable device and nutrition data. I aim to create scalable and interpretable tools that advance reproducibility, equity, and rigor in biomedical research.

Mission & Values

What drives me as a researcher is the belief that rigorous methods are essential to revealing the truth in data. My mission is to develop tools that not only improve the validity of biomedical findings but also make these tools accessible to the wider scientific and public health communities.

Personally, I value persistence, collaboration, and curiosity. As the first in my family to pursue higher education, I understand the importance of resilience and mentorship. I strive to contribute to a more equitable scientific community by developing methods that address biases in data and by mentoring the next generation of statisticians and data scientists.

Ultimately, I see my work as part of a broader effort to ensure that advances in data science and AI/ML lead to health solutions that are fair, reproducible, and impactful across diverse populations.

Research Interests

  • Measurement error correction in functional and high-dimensional data
  • Bayesian and corrected-score methods
  • Functional data analysis and wearable device data
  • Applications in nutrition, obesity, and chronic disease epidemiology
  • AI/ML methods for biomedical data
